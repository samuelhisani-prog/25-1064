Key features include

Full fairings for aerodynamics

Fairings are streamlined structures designed to reduce aerodynamic drag by smoothing out irregular surfaces, gaps, and protrusions on vehicles like aircraft and motorcycles. Their primary function is to allow air to flow more smoothly over the vehicle's surface, minimizing turbulence and resistance. By reducing drag, fairings improve fuel efficiency, increase speed, and enhance overall flight or riding performance.

Body work and carbon fiber

Carbon fiber bodywork is a popular aftermarket upgrade for sport bikes, valued for its lightweight construction and high strength-to-weight ratio, which enhances performance and aerodynamics.These parts are designed to reduce unsprung weight, improving acceleration and handling.

Braking

Motorcycle braking systems are designed to convert kinetic energy into thermal energy through friction, with the front brake typically responsible for approximately 70% of the braking effort due to weight transfer during deceleration. This front brake dominance is especially pronounced on sports bikes, which have a shorter wheelbase and more vertical fork geometry, allowing them to tolerate higher front braking load.

PRICING

New sport bikes generally cost between $8,000 and $22,000, with prices influenced by engine size, performance, and brand.
